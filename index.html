02
Deep Learning Architecture
HOW THE MODEL WORKS
📥
Data Pipeline
Raw cumulative COVID-19 counts from Johns Hopkins are converted to daily new cases, normalized to [0,1] using MinMaxScaler, and formed into 30-day sliding window sequences for supervised learning.
🧠
LSTM Architecture
A stacked 2-layer LSTM (128→64 units) with Dropout(0.2) regularization captures both short-term spikes and long-term wave trends. A Dense(32) + Dense(1) head outputs the next-day prediction.
⚙️
Training Strategy
Adam optimizer (lr=0.001), MSE loss, EarlyStopping (patience=15), and ReduceLROnPlateau (factor=0.5, patience=7) ensure the model converges optimally without overfitting on ~42 epochs.
📊
Evaluation
Test set evaluation using RMSE, MAE, MAPE, and R² metrics. Confusion of wave magnitude is analyzed through scatter plots comparing actual vs predicted values across all three pandemic waves.
🔮
30-Day Forecast
The trained model auto-regressively predicts 30 future days by feeding its own predictions back as inputs, with ±20% confidence bands to quantify forecast uncertainty over time.
💾
Outputs & Artifacts
All plots (EDA, training curves, predictions, scatter, forecast) are saved to an outputs/ folder. The best model weights are checkpointed as best_model.keras for reuse without retraining.

05
Real-World Motivation
THREE WAVES. ONE MODEL.
WAVE 01 — ORIGINAL STRAIN
MAR 2020 – JAN 2021
97,894
Peak daily cases on Sep 16, 2020. India's first national lockdown enforced. LSTM captures the gradual rise and exponential decay pattern.
WAVE 02 — DELTA VARIANT
MAR 2021 – JUN 2021
414,188
Deadliest surge. Hospital systems overwhelmed. Oxygen shortage crisis. Peak May 6, 2021. Delta's steep rise is the biggest challenge for LSTM forecasting.
WAVE 03 — OMICRON VARIANT
DEC 2021 – FEB 2022
347,254
Fast-spreading but lower fatality. Peak Jan 20, 2022. LSTM leverages Wave 1 & 2 patterns to partially anticipate the rapid onset of Wave 3.

This project is directly grounded in real decisions: governments used forecasting models exactly like this to plan ICU capacity, oxygen supply chains, vaccine distribution, and lockdown timing. The LSTM doesn't just predict numbers — it replicates the decision-support tools used during a global health emergency.
